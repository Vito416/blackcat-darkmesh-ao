# Registry Terms (Draft)

Status: active companion policy text for BFNL 1.0

These terms govern participation in the Official Registry, verified listings, and related trust or support programs.

## 1. Purpose

The Official Registry exists to:

- list gateways, sites, and shops that have completed the required registration steps;
- provide a transparent and auditable directory of participating operators;
- support trust, discovery, and operational coordination inside the Blackcat ecosystem.

## 2. Eligibility

A deployment may be eligible for a verified listing only if:

- the required Founder Fee has been paid under the current fee policy;
- Proof of Payment is valid;
- the operator can prove control over the relevant domain, gateway, or service identity if requested;
- the deployment is not in material breach of the applicable license, fee policy, or trademark policy;
- and any required technical metadata has been supplied truthfully.

## 3. Required registration data

The Registry operator may require:

- transaction id proving payment,
- domain name or gateway identifier,
- operator contact,
- deployment type,
- public verification data,
- and other reasonable metadata needed to maintain the Registry.

## 4. Nature of registry status

Verified listing means only that:

- the listed operator completed the stated registration and verification process to the satisfaction of the Registry operator at the time of listing.

Verified listing does not guarantee:

- legal compliance,
- uptime,
- security,
- fitness for purpose,
- or endorsement beyond the scope expressly described.

## 5. Official support and SLA

The Licensor or a validly designated Steward may offer support, SLA, or priority handling to verified operators.

Such benefits may depend on:

- the registration class,
- fee level,
- support plan,
- and continued compliance with the registry and fee policies.

## 6. Suspension and delisting

A listing may be suspended or removed if:

- the Founder Fee or any required Steward Fee is unpaid;
- Proof of Payment is invalid, misleading, or missing;
- the operator loses control of the relevant domain or deployment;
- the operator misuses Blackcat branding;
- the operator materially harms the security or integrity of the ecosystem;
- or the operator is otherwise in material breach of the governing terms.

Where practical, the Registry operator should provide notice and a cure period before permanent delisting, except where urgent security action is reasonably necessary.

Where urgent action is taken first, the Registry operator should provide a post-action explanation and a reasonable opportunity for review once the immediate risk has been addressed.

## 6A. Appeals and transparency log

The registry should, where practical, maintain a lightweight public log or equivalent public record of:

- listing approvals;
- suspensions;
- delistings;
- and materially relevant status changes.

Sensitive security details may be withheld where necessary, but the existence of a material action should ordinarily remain visible.

An operator materially affected by suspension or delisting should ordinarily have access to a reasonable review or appeal path, unless fraud, malicious conduct, impersonation, or urgent security circumstances make immediate permanent action reasonably necessary.

## 7. No entitlement to perpetual listing

Registry participation is a conditional service, not an irrevocable property right.

The Licensor or valid Steward may update listing rules, verification methods, or operational requirements prospectively, provided such changes are applied in good faith.

## 8. Public transparency

Because this ecosystem is designed around transparency and auditability:

- listing-related payment evidence may be publicly visible on Arweave;
- registry entries may expose domains, gateway identifiers, and public metadata;
- and verification status may be publicly queryable.

Operators are responsible for ensuring that any submitted contact or metadata is suitable for public disclosure if the registry process requires it.

## 9. Relationship to the code license

Registry participation does not expand the rights granted under the code license.

Likewise, having access to source code or non-production rights does not automatically create a right to verified listing or official support.

Conversely, registry rules, verified-status rules, trust-manifest rules, or support-program rules should not be interpreted to retroactively narrow an already validly acquired code-license scope for an ordinary covered deployment unless the governing license or fee policy clearly and prospectively creates a separate optional service class.

## 10. Relationship to trademarks

Use of official marks, verified labels, badges, or branding remains subject to the trademark policy.

Delisting or loss of verified status automatically ends any permission to use verified or official registry marks.

## 11. Future stewardship

If a valid Steward is designated by Founder Notice, the Steward may operate or administer the Registry in accordance with the governing license, fee policy, and trademark policy.

Unless the founder expressly states otherwise, such stewardship does not remove the founder's reserved Founder Fee.

Any material change in Registry operator identity should be publicly announced through an Authorized Notice Channel or equivalent public channel recognized by the governing license and fee policy.
