# Trademark Policy (Draft)

Status: draft for legal review

This document describes how names, logos, verified marks, and brand references connected with the Blackcat ecosystem may be used.

## 1. Scope

This policy covers, without limitation:

- `Blackcat`
- `Blackcat Mesh`
- `Blackcat Darkmesh`
- `Blackcat Mesh Nexus`
- associated logos, wordmarks, icons, badges, verified marks, and official registry labels

No trademark rights are granted by the source code license alone.

## 2. Ownership

All project names, marks, branding elements, and official verification badges are reserved to the Licensor unless and until the Licensor publishes a different written policy.

## 3. Allowed nominative use

You may use project names in a factual and descriptive way, for example:

- to say that your software is compatible with Blackcat,
- to refer to this repository,
- to discuss audits, integrations, or deployment guides,
- or to explain that your fork was originally derived from this codebase.

Such use must be truthful and must not imply sponsorship, certification, endorsement, or official status.

## 4. Prohibited use

Without explicit written permission, you may not:

- name a fork, hosted service, gateway, registry, or public deployment as if it were the official Blackcat network;
- use official logos, badges, or marks on a fork or derivative;
- describe your service as "official", "verified", "certified", or "supported" by Blackcat unless that status has been granted;
- use a confusingly similar name intended to imply continuity with the official network;
- or use branding in a way that suggests your deployment is part of the Official Registry when it is not.

## 5. Fork naming

Forks and modified versions are allowed only to the extent permitted by the applicable source-available license, but they must use distinct branding.

Recommended pattern:

- "Forked from Blackcat Darkmesh AO"
- "Based on Blackcat technology"

Not allowed:

- "Official Blackcat Gateway"
- "Blackcat Verified Shop"
- "Blackcat Mesh Registry"

unless expressly authorized in writing.

## 6. Verified and official marks

The following labels are reserved for official use:

- `Verified`
- `Official`
- `Certified`
- `Trusted by Blackcat`
- any equivalent mark, icon, or badge

Such labels may be used only by deployments listed in the Official Registry and in good standing under the applicable registry and fee policies.

## 7. Domain names and handles

You may not register, use, or promote a domain, social handle, or public identifier that is misleadingly similar to the official Blackcat brand in a way likely to confuse operators or end users.

This policy does not prohibit fair comparative commentary or truthful descriptive references.

## 8. Revocation

Any limited branding permission or verified listing status may be revoked if:

- the operator is no longer in compliance with the applicable license or fee policy;
- the operator misrepresents official status;
- the operator materially harms the trust or security of the ecosystem;
- or the operator uses the mark in a deceptive way.

## 9. Relationship to the license

This trademark policy is separate from the code license.

Even if source access or non-production rights are granted under the code license, no trademark rights are granted except as explicitly stated here or in a separate written authorization.
