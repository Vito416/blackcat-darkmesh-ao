# Dispute Resolution Framework

Status: active companion policy text for BFNL 1.0

This document describes the dispute-resolution structure for Blackcat Founder Network License 1.0 and its related policy documents.

## 1. Purpose

The purpose of this framework is to reduce future uncertainty about:

- governing law,
- forum,
- interpretive language,
- cure opportunities,
- evidence handling,
- and escalation paths before litigation or other formal proceedings.

## 2. Good-faith resolution first

The intended first step for ordinary disputes is good-faith resolution through notice and cure where reasonably possible.

Typical sequence:

1. operator notice,
2. clarification or evidence exchange,
3. cure opportunity,
4. proportional remediation,
5. and only then formal escalation if the dispute remains unresolved.

This is not meant to protect fraud, willful evasion, impersonation, or malicious conduct.

## 3. Governing law

The governing law is:

- the laws of the Slovak Republic,

excluding its conflict-of-law rules.

## 4. Authoritative language

The authoritative legal language of the licensing package is:

- English.

Translations may be published for convenience, but in case of conflict the English version controls.

## 5. Exclusive forum

The exclusive jurisdiction and venue model is:

- the courts located in Bratislava, Slovak Republic,
- or their lawful successor courts with subject-matter jurisdiction if the court structure is later reorganized.

The intended operational effect is that contract, licensing, trademark, registry, and contribution disputes should be heard in one predictable forum where legally possible.

## 6. No default arbitration layer

The default dispute model does not require a separate arbitration layer.

Arbitration may still be adopted later only by a later valid replacement license or other instrument expressly authorized to change the governing dispute terms.

## 7. Provisional relief and urgent cases

The dispute framework should preserve the right to seek urgent provisional relief for:

- active trademark misuse,
- impersonation,
- payment-address fraud,
- key compromise,
- malicious security abuse,
- or other urgent ecosystem harm.

That urgent path should coexist with, not destroy, the ordinary good-faith cure-first structure for non-malicious cases.

## 8. Evidence hierarchy

The intended dispute-evidence hierarchy should prioritize:

1. authenticated Founder Notices and succession notices,
2. signed fee schedules and notice-channel records,
3. Arweave or equivalent public proof records,
4. public market valuation references used by the fee policy,
5. registry status logs and appeal records,
6. and then other admissible supporting evidence.

## 9. Interpretation principles

Ambiguous terms should be interpreted, to the fullest extent permitted by law, consistently with:

- the Accessibility Principle,
- the Founder Accessibility Declaration,
- the Non-Exclusionary Enforcement Covenant,
- the order of precedence set by the governing license,
- and the project's stated intent to preserve both founder economics and practical accessibility.

## 10. Cure before damages for ordinary cases

For ordinary non-malicious operator disputes, the intended rule is:

- cure first where realistically available,
- damages or exclusion later if cure is refused or abused.

This does not bar claims for accrued payment obligations or recovery for deliberate breach.

## 11. Cost proportionality

The framework should avoid making ordinary fee disputes economically absurd by default.

If a disagreement is small and curable, the intended approach is proportional handling rather than immediate maximal-cost escalation.

## 12. Public-interest and micro-operator sensitivity

Where the disputed deployment is genuinely small, public-interest in nature, or otherwise economically fragile, the framework should be interpreted consistently with the Accessibility Principle and non-exclusionary enforcement structure.

This does not erase payment obligations, but it does support:

- cure windows,
- payment plans,
- hardship review,
- and other proportionate remedies.
